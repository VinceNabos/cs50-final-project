Overview

  • We all know the process of buying something. You pick an item, it has a price, you pay that price, and any extra amount will come back to you as change.
  • Now, we live in a world where calculating change is as easy as one, two, three! But even better, we can automate it.
  • In this exercise, you will implement a program that calculates the change of a customer.

Instructions

  • In Python, create a program that calculates the change of a customer.
  • The program will take two inputs, the price and the cash.
  • Your program must print() the change of the customer!

Test Cases

  • You can assume that all inputs will either be integers or floats.
  • You can assume that the cash variable will always be equal to or larger than the price.
  • Your printed output can be either integers or floats.
